Why I'm running for Mayor

I’m Steve - and I am motivated by a strong desire to see Roswell thrive under leadership that values citizen input, fiscal accountability, and responsible growth. That’s why I am now running for Roswell Mayor. I am committed to building a stronger Roswell — one that listens, adapts, and grows with its citizens.

As a Roswell resident and attorney for 42 years, I have a lengthy track record of serving our community and getting things done. A native of northern New York, near the Adirondacks, I moved here to begin my career with Arnold Golden Gregory LLP after earning my law degree from Duke University. Inspired by its charm and sense of community, I have actively contributed to Roswell's growth and well-being.

My nine years on Roswell City Council (1991-1999) were marked by results-driven initiatives:

  • Launched the nation’s most successful curbside recycling program and expanded the recycling center using cost-free land in Fulton County
  • Eliminated a $1 million deficit in the sanitation fund
  • Co-led the redevelopment and restoration of Canton Street and the historic district, including underground utilities and context-friendly street enhancements
  • Championed Parkway Village zoning to ensure green space and horse fences along Hwy 92
  • Secured land for Roswell Riverside Park and the donation of property for Leita Thompson Park
  • Promoted the joint police/fire training center on Hembree Road, saving millions of dollars while benefiting north Fulton cities

My focus on redevelopment—not rezoning—helped increased Roswell’s tax base by tens, if not hundreds, of millions of dollars. I was honored to be recognized by Creative Loafing Magazine as Editors’ Choice for Best Politician on the Northside.

Beyond council, I served as the North Fulton Representative on the Fulton County Library Board and Vice Chair, helping to make the East Roswell Library a reality while reforming the library system’s hiring practices. My commitment extends to local organizations where I’ve led homeowner associations in Pine Valley and Northcliff, served on the Roswell United Methodist Church Board of Trustees, and taught youth Sunday School for 20 years.

My community involvement also includes serving on the Children’s Development Academy (CDA) Board for 16 years (three as Chair), and chairing United Way campaigns that raised over $5 million.

My wife Ida and I have been married for 33 years, and we have two children, Lilla (29) and Russell (26). We enjoy traveling together, cheering for the Braves, Hawks, Falcons, a bunch of college football teams, and Duke basketball.
